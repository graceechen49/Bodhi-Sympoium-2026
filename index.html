The Different Pathways to a Total Portfolio Approach

Asset owners are increasingly shifting from the traditional Strategic Asset Allocation (SAA) framework to a Total Portfolio Approach (TPA) in pursuit of greater organizational agility and long-term performance. But is there a single, standard model for TPA? In this keynote address, Professor Sebastien Betermier draws on recent research to explore the various pathways to TPA and what the evidence reveals about this important evolution in portfolio management.
Dr. Sebastien Betermier, PhD | Associate Professor of Finance, McGill University; Executive Director, ICPM

Reading the Market at Scale: AI in Quant Equity

Traditional quantitative strategies often compress text into sentiment scores, losing the narrative context that fundamental analysts rely on. Large language models offer a fundamentally different approach — reading company narratives much like a skilled analyst, but across thousands of names simultaneously. Trevor Mottl examines how AI and LLMs are reshaping quantitative equity trading and research, highlighting key design principles, emerging opportunities, and practical limitations in this rapidly evolving field.
Trevor Mottl | Portfolio Manager, AI Quant Equity, Magnetar

NowTalk

Sizing Illiquidity at the Total Fund Level

Private assets offer diversification, illiquidity premiums, and access to alternative sources of alpha — making them a staple of sophisticated portfolios worldwide. Yet the risks embedded in these investments are notoriously difficult to quantify. This presentation explores alternative frameworks for measuring and understanding the true cost of illiquidity.
Chris Schindler, CFA | Strategy Lead, Systematic Strategy, Polar Asset Management Partners

Keynote

The Beauty of Mathematics: A Keynote with Manjul Bhargava

Manjul Bhargava is the only Canadian ever to win the Fields Medal and one of the most captivating and wide-ranging intellects of our time. A Princeton professor whose pioneering work in number theory has reshaped the field, Manjul also serves as the inaugural President of the National Museum of Mathematics (MoMath) in New York. He is equally celebrated for his extraordinary gift for revealing the beauty, magic, and universality of mathematics to audiences well beyond academia.
Dr. Manjul Bhargava, PhD (Fields Medal Winner) | Professor in Mathematics, Princeton University; President, National Museum of Mathematics (MoMath)

Keynote

The 7 Most Costly Words in Investing: "We Have Always Done It This Way"

What separates genuine high performance from the illusion of safety? Drawing on his work with institutional investors and hedge fund managers globally, Lawrence Evans delivers a keynote that challenges the systems and habits that often confuse complacency with safety. His presentation examines trader psychology, decision-making, and high-performance investing, highlighting the importance of self-awareness, questioning assumptions, distinguishing luck from skill, and fostering investment teams capable of genuine innovation.
Lawrence Evans | Director, Salomon Partners
